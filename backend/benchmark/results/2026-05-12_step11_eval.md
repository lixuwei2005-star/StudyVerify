## What did not work

The 60% → 75% anti-leak target was missed (final: 60.1%, essentially flat). Three honest findings:

- **Targeted topics improved (+8 to +30 pp) but don't dominate by hint volume.** prefix-sum, set, tree, recursion, linked-list all gained substantially. But these topics account for only ~25% of evaluated hints; larger-volume topics (math, string, array) didn't have constraints and didn't change.

- **Non-targeted topics regressed 4–7 pp.** May be LLM non-determinism over a 4-hour run, or a side-effect of the prompt rewrite touching the no-constraint default path. Worth a reproducibility re-run.

- **LLM judge is the binding constraint (65.5% flat across Step 9, 10, 11).** Phrase filter improved (62 entries vs 33) but became stricter as a measurement, not a production fix. The judge catches structural leakage phrase filters can't, and the judge's catch rate is unchanged.

The next quality frontier is LLM judge gap closure — either by tightening the judge prompt (asymmetric: stricter judge = more hints flagged but harder bar), retrying hint generation on judge-flag, or lowering hint generation temperature. These are deeper interventions than the anti-leak phrase/prompt work in Step 11.

## Why anti-leak combined didn't move

The Day 1 + Day 2 interventions targeted a specific failure mode — algorithmic-pattern topics leaking syntax. The data confirms they worked on that surface: every targeted topic moved up, sometimes substantially. So why is the aggregate flat?

1. **Phrase filter is now stricter** — 62 entries vs 33 — so passing both layers is mechanically harder. The phrase filter rate dropped 7 pp despite no model regression.
2. **Non-targeted topics regressed** — math, string, bit-manipulation each lost 4–8 pp, and they account for ~1,000 of the 1,447 hints. These topics didn't receive a per-topic constraint, but they share a prompt template that was rewritten in Day 2.5; the rewrite may have nudged the model toward slightly more directive language for non-algorithmic problems.
3. **The LLM judge is the binding constraint and it's flat** (65.5% → 65.5%). To move the combined rate up, we need to move the judge rate up, not just tighten the phrase filter.
